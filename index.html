You can call me Sam. My passions for anthropology, sociology, and daydreaming have blossomed into the service of others through UX Research and Accessibility Design.

My UX Skills

User Research

In order to immerse myself in the user’s perspective, I approach and define the problem by collecting, synthesizing, and testing qualitative data. My process starts with a competitor analysis to understand the pain points that are commonly acknowledged, I then conduct ethnographic studies, interviews and surveys. With this context, I can almost experience the user’s unmet needs and carry out task analysis.

User Empathy

As a problem solver, I invest in the effectiveness of my product by empathizing with the user, not just sympathizing with the problem. To humanize as many of the pain points found in the collected research, I create user personas, storyboards and user journeys, and empathy maps. Now my team and investors can personally identify with our product user in a more collaborative way.

Accessible Design

Making my designs inclusive for individuals across all cultures, ages, spectrums, and abilities is a priority; Digital products should enhance the quality of life. There is no reason the beauty and function of a design should be compromised for users with disabilities when there are accessibility checklists , design kits, automated testing tools, Responsive Design.
